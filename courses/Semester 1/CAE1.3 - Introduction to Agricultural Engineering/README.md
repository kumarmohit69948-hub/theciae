# CAE1.3 — Introduction to Agricultural Engineering

B.Tech. Agricultural Engineering (ICAR 6th Deans' Committee syllabus).

## Lecture plan

- **Lecture 1:** Agricultural Engineering as a discipline; Major divisions of Agricultural
- **Lecture 2:** Farm mechanization needs and strategy; Classification of farm machinery 3 on the basis of unit operations; Principles of selection of machinery for different sizes of land and matching power sources
- **Lecture 3:** Different types of equipment for tillage, sowing, planting and transplanting, 4 fertilizer application, weed control, and plant protection; Harvesting and threshing equipment for rice, wheat, maize, cotton, sugarcane, fruits, tuber crops and other locally important crops
- **Lecture 4:** Functions and capabilities of tractor and power tillers
- **Lecture 5:** Introduction to the IC engine systems, fuel and air supply systems, cooling 2 and lubricating systems, and electrical systems in a tractor
- **Lecture 6:** Basic parts of a power tiller; Hitching system
- **Lecture 7:** Introduction to renewable energy systems; Types of biogas plants
- **Lecture 8:** Windmills and their different parts
- **Lecture 9:** Importance of soil and water conservation; Different agronomic measures 3 for control of water erosion, mixed cropping, crop rotation, tillage practices, mulching; Different engineering measures; gully control measures
- **Lecture 10:** Use of topographical survey and contour maps Different types of water 2 harvesting structures
- **Lecture 11:** Introduction to soil-plant-water relationship; Equipment for measurement 2 of irrigation water, viz. weirs, notches, orifices and mouthpieces
- **Lecture 12:** Introduction to different surface irrigation methods as border, furrow and 3 check basin, sprinkler, drip irrigation and their different components
- **Lecture 13:** Underground water conveyance methods in pipes
- **Lecture 14:** Introduction to planning of drainage systems
- **Lecture 15:** Introduction to centrifugal pumps and different components
- **Lecture 16:** Different types of agricultural structures; Introduction to planning and 2 layout of farmsteads, animal houses, poultry houses
- **Lecture 18:** Classification of different types of agricultural commodities as durables, 2 perishables, etc.; moisture content and its importance in grain storage
- **Lecture 19:** Different primary processing operations and their necessity; Methods and 2 equipment used for cleaning, washing, sorting, grading, peeling, size reduction
- **Lecture 20:** Different types of traditional and modern storage structures; Storage of 2 perishable commodities
- **Lecture 21:** Different types of packaging materials and their suitability for various food 2 products
- **Lecture 22:** Basic principles of value addition of food as drying and dehydration, 3 evaporation, thermal processing, refrigerated and frozen storage, chemical preservation and other novel methods
