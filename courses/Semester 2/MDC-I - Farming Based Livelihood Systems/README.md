# MDC-I — Farming Based Livelihood Systems

B.Tech. Agricultural Engineering (ICAR 6th Deans' Committee syllabus).

## Lecture plan

- **Lecture 1:** Status of agriculture in India and different states, Income of farmers and 3 rural people in India, Livelihood-Definition, concept and livelihood pattern in urban & rural areas. Different indicators to study livelihood systems
- **Lecture 2:** Agricultural livelihood systems (ALS): Meaning, approach, approaches 3 and framework, Definition of farming systems and farming based livelihood systems, Prevalent Farming systems in India contributing to livelihood
- **Lecture 4:** Horticultural crops, Agro--forestry systems, Aqua culture Duck/Poultry 4 cum Fish, Dairy cum Fish, Piggery cum Fish etc., Small, medium and large enterprises including value chains and secondary enterprises as livelihood components for farmers
- **Lecture 5:** Factors affecting the integration of various enterprises of farming for 2 livelihood
- **Lecture 6:** Feasibility of different farming systems for different agro-climatic zones
- **Lecture 7:** Case studies on different livelihood enterprises associated with the 4 farming
- **Lecture 8:** Risk & success factors in farming based livelihood systems, Schemes & 4 programmes by Central & State Government, Public & Private organisations involved in the promotion of farming based livelihood opportunities
- **Lecture 9:** Role of farming based livelihood enterprises in the 21st Century in view 4 of circular economy, green economy, climate change, digitalization & changing life style
