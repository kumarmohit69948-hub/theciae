# PFE3.7 — Engineering Properties of Agricultural Produce and Food Science

B.Tech. Agricultural Engineering (ICAR 6th Deans' Committee syllabus).

## Lecture plan

- **Lecture 1:** Different engineering properties of food and their importance; Application 2 of engineering properties in handling, processing and storage
- **Lecture 2:** Physical properties, viz. shape, size, roundness, sphericity, volume, density, 4 porosity, specific gravity, surface area; Colour properties, CIE colour model
- **Lecture 3:** Thermal properties, viz. heat capacity, specific heat, thermal conductivity, 4 thermal diffusivity, heat of respiration, co-efficient of thermal expansion
- **Lecture 4:** Frictional properties, viz. static friction, kinetic friction, rolling resistance, 4 angle of internal friction, angle of repose, flow of bulk granular materials
- **Lecture 5:** Rheological characteristics of food, elastic, plastic and viscous behaviour, 6 visco-elasticity; rheological models to explain food characteristics; Fluid behaviour as Newtonian, non-Newtonian, pseudo-plastic, dilatant, thixotropic, rheopectic and Bingham plastic; Textural characteristics of foods
- **Lecture 6:** Non-destructive methods of quality determination of foods; Principles of 4 machine vision systems, spectroscopy, hyperspectral imaging and acoustic techniques
- **Lecture 7:** Introduction to food science and food technology; Biochemical reactions 4 involved in food processing and storage; Food spoilage agents, general methods for food preservation (physical, chemical and biological methods)
- **Lecture 8:** Food microbiology: Classification of microorganisms, multiplication of 4 bacteria, Different beneficial and harmful microorganisms in relation to food preservation and spoilage, industrial bacteriology and food fermentation
