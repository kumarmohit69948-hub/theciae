# FMPE5.4 — Tractor and Automotive Engines

B.Tech. Agricultural Engineering (ICAR 6th Deans' Committee syllabus).

## Lecture plan

- **Lecture 1:** Sources of farm power: conventional and non-conventional energy sources
- **Lecture 2:** Classification of tractors and IC engines
- **Lecture 3:** Review of thermodynamic principles of IC (CI and SI) engines and deviation 2 from ideal cycle; General energy equation and heat balance sheet
- **Lecture 4:** Derivation of thermal efficiency of Otto cycle, Diesel cycle and Dual cycle
- **Lecture 6:** Engine strokes and comparison of 2-stroke and 4-stroke engine cycles and CI 1 and SI engines
- **Lecture 7:** Engine valve systems, valve mechanism, valve timing diagram, valve 3 clearance adjustment; Cam profile, valve lift and valve opening area
- **Lecture 8:** Inlet and exhaust systems; Importance of air cleaning system; Types of air 2 cleaners and performance characteristics of various air cleaners
- **Lecture 9:** Fuel supply system, types of fuels, properties of fuels, calculation of air-fuel 4 ratio. Different tests on fuel for SI and CI engines; Detonation and knocking in IC engines; Carburetion system, carburetors and their main functional components; Fuel injection system- injection pump, their types, working principles; Fuel injector nozzles- types and working principles
- **Lecture 10:** Engine governing- need of governors, governor types and governor 2 characteristics
- **Lecture 11:** Lubrication system- need, types, functional components; Lubricants- physical 3 properties, additives and their application
- **Lecture 12:** Engine cooling system- need, cooling methods and main functional 3 components; Need and types of thermostat valves; Additives in the coolant
- **Lecture 13:** Ignition system of SI engines; Electrical system including battery, starting 3 motor, battery charging, cut-out, etc.; Comparison of dynamo and alternator
- **Lecture 14:** Basics of engine testing
