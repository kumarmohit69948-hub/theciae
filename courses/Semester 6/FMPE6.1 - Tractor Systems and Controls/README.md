# FMPE6.1 — Tractor Systems and Controls

B.Tech. Agricultural Engineering (ICAR 6th Deans' Committee syllabus).

## Lecture plan

- **Lecture 1:** Transmission system- need of the system in a tractor, types, major functional 1 systems
- **Lecture 2:** Clutch- need, types, functional requirements, construction and principle of 3 operation; Single plate, multi-plate, centrifugal and dual clutch systems
- **Lecture 4:** Differential system- need, functional components, construction, calculation for 2 speed reduction; Final drive
- **Lecture 5:** Brake system- types, principle of operation, construction, calculation for 2 braking torque
- **Lecture 6:** Steering system- requirements, steering geometry characteristics, functional 3 components, calculation for turning radius; Ackerman steering; Steering systems in track type tractors
- **Lecture 7:** Hydraulic system- principle of operation, types, main functional components, 3 functional requirements. hydraulic system adjustments and ADDC
- **Lecture 8:** Tractor power outlets- PTO standards, types and functional requirements
- **Lecture 9:** Traction- traction terminology, theoretical calculation of shear force and 2 rolling resistance of traction device
- **Lecture 10:** Wheels and tyres- solid tyres and pneumatic tyres, tyre construction and tyre 2 specifications; Traction aids
- **Lecture 11:** Tractor mechanics- forces acting on the tractor
- **Lecture 12:** Determination of CG of a tractor, importance and determination of moment of 2 inertia of a tractor, tractor static equilibrium, tractor stability especially at turns
- **Lecture 13:** Maximum drawbar pull and its determination; Tractor as a spring-mass system
- **Lecture 14:** Ergonomic considerations and operational safety
- **Lecture 15:** Tractor testing; Tractor and Engine test codes
