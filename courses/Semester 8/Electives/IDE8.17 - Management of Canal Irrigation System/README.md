# IDE8.17 — Management of Canal Irrigation System

B.Tech. Agricultural Engineering (ICAR 6th Deans' Committee syllabus).

## Lecture plan

- **Lecture 1:** Typical network of canal irrigation system and its physical components
- **Lecture 3:** Canal alignment: general considerations
- **Lecture 4:** Different parts of canal sections, performance indicators for canal 3 irrigation system evaluation
- **Lecture 5:** Estimation of water requirements for canal command areas and 2 determination of canal capacity
- **Lecture 6:** Base period, water duty and delta, relationship between base period, duty 2 and delta; factors affecting duty and method to improve duty
- **Lecture 7:** Silt theory: Kennedy's theory, design of channels by Kennedy's theory
- **Lecture 8:** Maintenance of unlined irrigation canals, measurement of discharge in 2 canals
- **Lecture 9:** Rostering (canal running schedule) and warabandi, rotational irrigation, 2 pre-requisite of warabandi
- **Lecture 10:** Necessity of canal lining: advantages and disadvantages, types of canal 3 lining and desirable characteristics for the suitability of lining materials, design of lined canals
- **Lecture 11:** Functions of distributary head and cross regulators
- **Lecture 12:** Canal falls, their necessity and factors affecting canal fall, types of canal 2 falls
- **Lecture 13:** Sources of surplus water in canals and types of canal escapes
- **Lecture 14:** Requirements of a good canal outlet and types of outlet
- **Lecture 15:** Participatory irrigation management (PIM), water user's association: 2 necessity, structure, function and duties
