# PFE8.37 — Natural Fiber Applications in Agriculture

B.Tech. Agricultural Engineering (ICAR 6th Deans' Committee syllabus).

## Lecture plan

- **Lecture 1:** Natural Fiber based Agrotextiles, Characterization and their application
- **Lecture 2:** Overview of Agrotextile, Technical Textile, Non-woven technology, Design 2 principles for Natural Fiber based Agrotextiles
- **Lecture 3:** Tensile and Hydro-physical properties, Estimation techniques, Application as 3 crop mulch, Effect on soil properties, Measurement of soil hydrothermal regime, Effects on crop yield, Effects on soil moisture retention and weed population
- **Lecture 4:** Application as shade net, Biodegradability, Life cycle and environmental 1 impact, Economic evaluation
- **Lecture 5:** Natural Fiber based Geotextiles, Characterization and their application:
- **Lecture 6:** Tensile and Hydro-physical properties, Estimation techniques, Application as 4 soil saver, Effect on soil degradation properties, Factors affecting soil erosion
- **Lecture 7:** Concept of integrated watershed management and role of RS and GIS
- **Lecture 8:** Natural Fiber based sustainable packaging for Agricultural/Horticultural 2 produce: Overview of the packaging industry and current packaging materials
- **Lecture 10:** Advantages and limitations of natural fiber-based packaging compared to 3 synthetic alternatives, Biodegradable plastics for packaging agricultural produce. Life cycle assessment and environmental impact analysis
- **Lecture 11:** Potential of natural fibers as a source of renewable energy: Characteristics of 2 natural fibers and their suitability as a source of renewable energy, Methods of converting natural fibers into energy, such as combustion and gasification
- **Lecture 12:** Economic and environmental analysis of natural fiber-based renewable energy 3 systems, Comparison of natural fiber-based energy systems with other renewable energy sources, Government policies and incentives related to renewable energy (Bioethanol)
- **Lecture 13:** Role of Natural Fiber in Organic and Soilless Farming: Overview of organic 2 and natural farming, soilless farming, natural fiber waste, characterization of waste, different methods of compost preparation, role of fiber waste as compost
- **Lecture 14:** Characteristics of natural fibers and their suitability as a source of soilless 2 media, Effect on crop yield, Biodegradability, Life cycle and environmental impact, Economic evaluation
