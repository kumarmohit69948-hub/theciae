# SWCE8.13 — Remote Sensing and GIS Applications

B.Tech. Agricultural Engineering (ICAR 6th Deans' Committee syllabus).

## Lecture plan

- **Lecture 1:** Basic component of remote sensing (RS), advantages and limitations of RS
- **Lecture 2:** Possible use of RS techniques in assessment and monitoring of land and 1 water resources
- **Lecture 3:** Electromagnetic spectrum, energy interactions in the atmosphere and with 2 the Earth's surface
- **Lecture 4:** Major atmospheric windows, principal applications of different wavelength regions, typical spectral reflectance curve for vegetation, soil and water
- **Lecture 5:** Different types of sensors and platforms, contrast ratio and possible causes 2 of low contrast
- **Lecture 7:** Air-photo interpretation- interpretation elements
- **Lecture 8:** Photogrammetry- measurements on a single vertical aerial photograph, measurements on a stereo-pair- vertical measurement by the parallax 2 method; Ground control for aerial photography
- **Lecture 9:** Satellite remote sensing, multispectral scanner whiskbroom and push-broom 2 scanner
- **Lecture 10:** Different types of resolutions
- **Lecture 11:** Analysis of digital data- image restoration; image enhancement
- **Lecture 12:** Information extraction, image classification, unsupervised classification, supervised classification, important consideration in the identification of 2 training areas
- **Lecture 13:** Vegetation indices
- **Lecture 14:** Microwave remote sensing
- **Lecture 15:** GIS and basic components
- **Lecture 16:** Different sources of spatial data, basic spatial entities, major components of 2 spatial data
- **Lecture 17:** Basic classes of map projections and their properties
- **Lecture 18:** Methods of data input into GIS, data editing
- **Lecture 19:** Spatial data models and structures, attribute data management, integrating 1 data (map overlay) in GIS
- **Lecture 20:** Application of remote sensing and GIS for the management of land and 1 water resources
